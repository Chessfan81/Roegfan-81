📊 Projects
1. Homicide in the USA (1980–2014)
A data-driven investigation into one of America's most dramatic social transformations — analysed across three layers: statistical, sociological, and geographic.
Dataset: FBI Supplementary Homicide Reports (Kaggle) · 638,000 records · fused with US Census population data
Methods & Tools:
Time series forecasting: ARIMA(1,1,1), ARIMA(0,1,1), Holt's Linear Trend, Facebook Prophet
Stationarity testing: Augmented Dickey-Fuller, ACF/PACF analysis
Geographic visualisation: animated choropleth, bump chart (state rankings)
Population-adjusted rate analysis
Python · Pandas · Plotly · Statsmodels · Prophet · Jupyter
Key Findings:
Population-adjusted homicide rate fell 55% from 10 per 100,000 (1980) to 4.4 (2014)
Prophet outperformed ARIMA — RMSE 0.28 vs 0.49 — due to superior changepoint detection
White and Black homicide rates follow near-identical trajectories, pointing to systemic macro-sociological drivers rather than race-specific factors
The national decline is geographically concentrated — Southern states show flat or rising trends
